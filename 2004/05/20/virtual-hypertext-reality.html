Virtual Hypertext Reality

Right now, I start reading Mary Laure Ryan "Narrative as Virtual Reality" again. This enormous Book is from a time, when internet was young and the humanities were keen on dicovering the new medium for themselves. As I have coped with the Internet and Hypertext for the last two years only in terms of HTML, CSS, XML, XTM, RDF, XSLT SVG and so on, I forgot, that these humanities-centered perspektives exist.

In the Introduction of the book, she give a brief overview on the history of humanities and hytertext. She lists up the - to me well know - big Names - Landow, Bolter, Joyce, Moulthrop - and their ideas concerning hypertext and other big names - Barthes, Derrida, Delleuze Guattari, Foucault. She tells the story of, how hypertext became a the medium of postmodern theory.

It's not so, that this story is new to me. I fact I know i quite well. I've read Landow, Bolter and Joyce. I read Derrida and Deleuze and I really liked their Ideas.

But since I read Aarseths "Cybertext" and Illichs "In the Vineyard of the Text" those postmodern ideas don't seem to be apporiate to descibe new media to me. I discarded them for my work. And now reading about these ideas after two years of concentrated work on other aspects of media, they look very strange to me, just as Hugo de St.Victors notion of text in the 12th century.

And I wonder, who new and old media will actually evolve during my lifetime.
