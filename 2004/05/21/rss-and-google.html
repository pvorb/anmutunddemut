RSS and Google

When google became the most popular search engine, wors spread, that google should work like this: The more other sites link to you with a "special string" as the content of the a-element in these link, the higher your positon in the result list for this "special string" gets.

Watching the statistics of my weblog, I got the suspicion, that google changed their tactic during the last months/years: actually nobody is linking this weblog with other linktitles than "buddhabot"
or "ben". But since I got an RSS feed on my site, and since I linked
very weblogentry from the startpage, I got about 75 hits per day.

Ok ok, since my weblog is the startpage of my browser some of these hits are me. But when I look at the searchstrings, by which people come [mostly by mistake] to my page, I'm suprised every day on how high my googleranking ist for some searchstrings.

I wonder, what happends, if i annotate the whole page with RDF...
