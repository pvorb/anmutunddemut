Here we go! XHTML 2.0

Still XHTML 2.0 has the status of a working draft,
what means, that the people who desing this documentclass and its connections to other documentclass have to discuss wuite a lot until someone starts to implement it. But what's already oon the horizont is, that XHTML 2.0 is not only another cut down, semantiv, xmlized version of HTML. XHTML 2.0 seems to get some additional elements, which describe functional aspects of todays websites:

section
is just like the div element an almost generic element to mark sections of other block-level-elements. But it is only meant for content; not for navigation or orientation. It should be use in combination with the new h element.
h
is an unorderd heading. h gets its meaning by its position inside different section elemten. This is kinda close to doc:book standard.
This will make desingen with css a bit harder as you will hav to define different header by their depth inside sections. But it makes XSLT transformations on XHTML 2.0 documents far easier, as the parrallel structre of HTML headings and blockelements is hell for an
XSLT-Programmer.
nl
is an navigation-list, which is a VERY important addition to HTML, as navigations are the most important an widely used addition to
semantic of websites. Right now there is no way for user agent (browser) to devide between content of a webpage and navigation.

I my opinion an orientation element should be added also as orientation is the third most important functional element of a webpage - after content and navigation. A fourth and fith important
elemt might be "footer/impress" and "logo/title". But as I'm not part of the working group, i guess my opinion is not that important.
