The Semantic Weblog

The Semantic Weblog actually is a pretty simple idea. While you are blogging, you continually write about repeating topics. This continuity is usually represented by keywords and tags. Many of these keyword have a strong lexical notion, which means that the word is pretty likely to be found in the wikipedia. So an avarage blogger creates a small Lexikon next to his (more journalistic) articles. To get a real Lexikon you only two things are missing: 1. Texts explaining the Terms and 2. References between the Terms.

Lexical Articles

Drupal already offers a function to add a description to a term. But Terms do not share the same Properties as real Articles, Nodes. They can't be found by the search, can't be commented and so on. This is ok, because they are not meant to be content.

But why should any blogger want to write lexical articles in the first place, as the wikipedia is already pretty great? The Wikipedia does and will not cover all parts of our lifes. There is a relevance test for every new article, and many topics espacially of our every-day culture will never pass this test. But there is a more important fact: The Wikipedia doesn't have an opinion. The Articles on the Wikipedia have to be from an objective point of view. But opinion is the big strength of Blogs. And many users are deeply interersted in optinion of People on Topics.

Even if i had to write a lexical article on Drupal, XML or the Semantic Web it would never appeare on the Wikipedia in that form.

Typed Relations

There's one reason why the Semantic Weblog ist not called "Lexical Weblog": The Semantic Weblog allows to create typed relations between lexical articles and not only links. This has a special reason. Every lexical article represents a concept or a topic of the real world. Regular Node-Types, like Story, Image, Quote and so on also cope with topics. But the do not represent them. Concepts in the real world are connected in extremly, extremly, extremely interesting Relations, like Supertopic-Subtopic, Whole-Part, Father-Son, Creator-Creation, Opposite, Synonym. Actually there is a scientific school that argues, that these typed relations form the basis of our undertanding of the World.

These realtions are not only interesting to follow for reader of a blog. They also allow to draw conclusion: If "Content Mangement System" is the supertopic for "Drupal" then someone searching für "Content Management System" propably is interested in Articles on "Drupal as well."

These typed relations called "Trippel" form the backbone of the Semantic Web. RDF - the now established Markuplanguage of the semantic Web - is hardly more than Triples of URLs. When we store these relations between lexical articles, we can always export these data as RDF an by this become an active part of the Semantic Web.
